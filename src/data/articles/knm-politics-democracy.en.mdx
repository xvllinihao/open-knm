      <h2>What kind of democracy does the Netherlands have?</h2>
      <p>
        The Netherlands is a <strong>parliamentary democracy (parlementaire democratie)</strong> and a{" "}
        <strong>constitutional monarchy (constitutionele monarchie)</strong>. Citizens elect representatives and there is a King or Queen whose powers are limited by the Constitution.
      </p>
      <p>
        The <strong>parliament (parlement)</strong> has two chambers: the{" "}
        <strong>House of Representatives (Tweede Kamer)</strong> and the{" "}
        <strong>Senate (Eerste Kamer)</strong>. The Tweede Kamer is directly elected and has the main powers to make laws and control the government.
      </p>

      <h2>The King, the government and the Prime Minister</h2>
      <p>
        The <strong>government (regering)</strong> is made up of the{" "}
        <strong>King (Koning)</strong> and the <strong>ministers (ministers)</strong>. Day‑to‑day policy is carried out by the{" "}
        <strong>cabinet (kabinet)</strong>, which includes all ministers and state secretaries.
      </p>
      <p>
        The head of the cabinet is the <strong>Prime Minister (minister-president)</strong>, not the King. The cabinet is accountable to the{" "}
        <strong>Tweede Kamer</strong>; if it loses the confidence of parliament, it may have to resign.
      </p>
