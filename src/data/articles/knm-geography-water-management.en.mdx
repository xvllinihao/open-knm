    <div className="space-y-4">
      <h2>1. Water and Safety</h2>
      <p>
        The Netherlands is a flat country. A large part of the country lies below sea level (<strong>onder de zeespiegel</strong>).
      </p>
      <p>
        Therefore, the Netherlands must protect itself. Without <strong>dikes (dijken)</strong> and <strong>dunes (duinen)</strong>, part of the Netherlands would flood.
      </p>
      <p>
        The <strong>dikes</strong> are high to keep the land safe from the water. The Netherlands lies on the <strong>North Sea (Noordzee)</strong>.
        (Note: The Netherlands uses **NAP** as the standard for measuring water levels).
      </p>

      <h2>2. Polders and Water Board</h2>
      <p>
        The Dutch have created a lot of new land. This land that used to be water is called <strong>Polders</strong>. Creating new land is called <strong>inpolderen</strong>.
      </p>
      <p>
        <strong>Flevoland</strong> is a province made entirely of a <strong>Polder</strong>.
      </p>
      <p>
        The <strong>Water Board (Waterschap)</strong> is an organization that manages the water in the Netherlands. You pay <strong>water authority tax (waterschapsbelasting)</strong> to maintain the dikes and clean water.
      </p>

      <h2>3. The Randstad</h2>
      <p>
        The <strong>Randstad</strong> is the busy west of the Netherlands. The four largest cities are located in the <strong>Randstad</strong>: <strong>Amsterdam</strong>, <strong>Rotterdam</strong>, <strong>The Hague</strong>, and <strong>Utrecht</strong>.
      </p>
      <p>
        Half of all Dutch people live and work in the <strong>Randstad</strong>. There is a lot of work there. But there are often <strong>traffic jams (files)</strong> on the highways.
      </p>

      <h2>4. Provinces and Capital</h2>
      <p>
        The Netherlands has <strong>12 provinces</strong>.
      </p>
      <p>
        <strong>Amsterdam</strong> is the <strong>capital</strong> of the Netherlands, located in <strong>North Holland (Noord-Holland)</strong>. The <strong>capital</strong> of North Holland is <strong>Haarlem</strong>.
      </p>
      <p>
        However, the government is located in <strong>The Hague (Den Haag)</strong>.
      </p>
