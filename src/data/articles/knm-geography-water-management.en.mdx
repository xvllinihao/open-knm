      <h2>The Netherlands: a country below sea level</h2>
      <p>
        A large part of the Netherlands lies <strong>below sea level</strong>. Without
        protection, many areas would flood regularly. That is why there are{" "}
        <strong>dikes (dijken)</strong>, canals and{" "}
        <strong>pumping stations (gemalen)</strong> everywhere to remove excess water.
      </p>
      <p>
        A <strong>polder</strong> is land that has been drained and is surrounded by dikes. The
        water level is controlled by engineering works and is often managed by{" "}
        <strong>water boards (waterschappen)</strong>, regional authorities responsible for
        water management.
      </p>

      <h2>Delta Works: from disaster to protection</h2>
      <p>
        In 1953 the Netherlands suffered a major flood, the{" "}
        <strong>North Sea flood (Watersnoodramp van 1953)</strong>. After this disaster the
        Dutch started building the <strong>Delta Works (Deltawerken)</strong>, including large
        dams and <strong>storm surge barriers (stormvloedkeringen)</strong>.
      </p>
      <p>
        Well‑known examples are the <strong>Oosterscheldekering</strong> and the{" "}
        <strong>Maeslantkering</strong>. In extreme storms or very high tides they can be closed
        to protect the land behind them from the sea.
      </p>
