      <h2>From primary to secondary: the tracks</h2>
      <p>
        Children in the Netherlands usually start{" "}
        <strong>primary school (basisschool)</strong> at the age of four. From the age of five
        until at least sixteen they are under{" "}
        <strong>compulsory education (leerplicht)</strong>, and parents must make sure they
        attend school.
      </p>
      <p>
        After primary school, pupils move into different secondary tracks:{" "}
        <strong>vmbo</strong>, <strong>havo</strong> or <strong>vwo</strong>. These are very
        common words in Dutch education and you will often hear them when people talk about
        school levels.
      </p>

      <h2>VMBO / HAVO / VWO, MBO / HBO / university</h2>
      <p>
        <strong>VMBO</strong> is more practical and usually leads to{" "}
        <strong>vocational education (MBO)</strong>. <strong>HAVO</strong> is more theoretical
        and often leads to <strong>universities of applied sciences (HBO)</strong>.{" "}
        <strong>VWO</strong> is the most academic track and prepares for{" "}
        <strong>university (universiteit)</strong>.
      </p>
      <p>
        These tracks are <strong>different ways and speeds of learning</strong>, not a value
        judgment about people. Ideally, everyone finds the track that best fits their talents.
      </p>

        which level or phase they refer to and whether they are more practical or theoretical.
