      <h2>Common employment contract types</h2>
      <p>
        In the Netherlands an employment contract is called an{" "}
        <strong>arbeidsovereenkomst</strong>. Common types are the{" "}
        <strong>temporary contract (tijdelijk contract)</strong>, the{" "}
        <strong>permanent contract (vast contract)</strong> without an end date, and the{" "}
        <strong>on‑call contract (oproepcontract)</strong>, where you work only when called.
      </p>
      <p>
        In general a <strong>permanent contract (vast)</strong> offers more security than a{" "}
        <strong>temporary one (tijdelijk)</strong>. However, even temporary or on‑call workers
        are protected by rules on <strong>minimum wage (minimumloon)</strong> and{" "}
        <strong>working conditions (cao, arbeidsvoorwaarden)</strong>.
      </p>

      <h2>Probation and dismissal protection</h2>
      <p>
        Many contracts start with a <strong>probation period (proeftijd)</strong>. During this
        short time both employer and employee can more easily end the contract to see if it is a
        good match.
      </p>
      <p>
        After the probation period, Dutch law offers{" "}
        <strong>dismissal protection (ontslagbescherming)</strong>. An employer cannot simply
        say &quot;don&apos;t come back tomorrow&quot;; a valid reason and approval by an
        authority or court are usually required.
      </p>

      <h2>Why is this important for KNM?</h2>

        should recognise that the question is about contract length and job security, not
        something completely new.
