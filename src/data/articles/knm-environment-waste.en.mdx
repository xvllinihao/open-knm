  <EnglishColumn title="1. Separating waste">
    <p>
      In the Netherlands, you must **separate waste (afval scheiden)**. This is better for the environment because many materials can be reused.
    </p>
    <p>
      There are different types of waste:
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li>**GFT** (Vegetable, Fruit, and Garden waste): this goes in the green bin.</li>
      <li>**Paper (Papier)** and cardboard: this goes in the paper bin.</li>
      <li>**Glass (Glas)**: you take this to the glass recycling container.</li>
      <li>**Chemical waste (Chemisch afval)** (such as batteries, paint, and medicines): hand this in separately at a collection point or recycling center.</li>
      <li>**Residual waste (Restafval)**: everything that remains goes in the grey bin.</li>
    </ul>
  </EnglishColumn>

  <EnglishColumn title="2. Rules and fines">
    <p>
      Every municipality has its own rules. You can check the **waste calendar (afvalkalender)** to see on which day the garbage is collected.
    </p>
    <p>
      If you put the garbage out on the wrong day or do not separate it correctly, you can get a **fine (boete)** from the municipality.
    </p>
